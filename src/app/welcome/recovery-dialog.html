Recovery Actions

  1. First, ensure your internet connection is working. If you have a good connection and have previously used Quick Crypt, select the passkey you used when you first signed in. You may have created a passkey with a physical key, a mobile device, or a password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password.
  2. If you lost or damaged your passkeys, take one of these actions:
    • Restore access to your account using the recovery words provided when you created your account. Just follow the link above and enter your recovery word pattern.
    • If you created an account before Quick Crypt switched to recovery words, locate the recovery link that was provided when you created your account. Then paste that link into your browser's address bar and press Enter.
  3. If you lost both your Quick Crypt passkeys and recovery information, you cannot access your existing user identity to decrypt or encrypt data. To continue using Quick Crypt, you may create a new account. If you find your original recovery information or passkey, you can use either to regain access to your account and decrypt data.
  4. If you found a problem with Quick Crypt, please report the issue.
