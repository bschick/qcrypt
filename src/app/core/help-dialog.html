Quick Crypt Help

Quick Crypt encrypts and decrypts text using a password you provide. Quick Crypt uses cryptographic features built into modern browsers and ensures that your password, clear text, and even encrypted cipher armor do not unexpectedly traverse the internet. After loading Quick Crypt, you could disable all networking and continue encrypting and decypting text. A typical use is to encrypt text before storing or sending it through an untrusted channel. Your original text can only be retrieved by someone who knows the password you used during encryption. Remember your password, or you won't be able to retrieve the original text either.

To use Quick Crypt, paste or load the text you want to encrypt into the field on the left labeled Enter Clear Text, then click the Encrypt button and enter a password. You can enter an optional password hint to remind yourself which password you used. The hint is stored in the clear with the encrypted text, so don't make it too revealing. The encrypted results are displayed to the right and can be copied or saved to disk. To decrypt information previously encrypted with Quick Crypt, paste or load the encrypted armor text in the field on the right labeled Enter Cipher Armor and click the Decrypt button. If you've forgotten your password, you are out of luck unless you have access to a supercomputer and lots of time to spare.

You can customize encryption options within the Advanced Encryptions Options panel. Other than your password choice, Quick Crypt does not provide options known to be insecure, so adjusting settings is safe. If you want even more security, you can encrypt clear text multiple times with the Loop Encrypt option; just be sure to use a different password for each loop.

Technical Details
  • Cryto Library: Web Crypto API and Libsodium
  • Key Derivation: PBKDF2 using SHA-512 and selectable iterations (default 0.5s of hashing or 400K)
  • Key Length: 256 bits (max for web crypto)
  • Encryption: AES with selectable mode of operation and optional HMAC-SHA256
  • Random Data: random.org or window.crypto.getRandomValues
  • Site Security: Mozilla Observatory A+ rating
